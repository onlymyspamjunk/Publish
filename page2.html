That's quite possibly why the parking along the drive is parallel to the curb instead of at an angle. Questionable though because it depends on length of a parked vehicle to clear that 20 feet. And it seems like it should clear that 20 feet... Some measurements need to be made to clarify this and to post the test results. But at the same time this totally does not explain why there is a fire lane no parking painted on the curb AFTER the mailbox after just 2 parking spots there. Because the distance from buildings to cars there is almost whopping 30 feet! Many cars could be parked there, LEGALLY. So it is really puzzling as to why the parking is banned over there.

Look at the pictures below: All the space behind these two cars is marked "NO PARKING FIRE LANE", even though the two spots where they "permitted" parking are NOT farther away than the spaces after those cars where they "banned" parking.

Our local architect that helps us said it is unlawful for a developer not to provide enough parking when they can. Actually by the common architectural standards this complex was supposed to have only 20 units instead of 28. So if the City gave the permission to the developer to build more than they were supposed to here, the developer could at least allow using the remaining space for parking, so much needed parking.

Gates just keep on breaking time after time

Gates are breaking rather frequently.

Some folks said that during the whole “life” of these gates they were broken 5 times. But 3 times can be confirmed for sure by most of us because it took forever to fix them. The issue seems to be with the gates just being too heavy for the parts that are attached to the gates to open them. On pictures from the news section of this site that depict gates being broken it can be clearly seen that the metal is just being torn out by the arms that open the gates.

The other thing that is making the issue worse is that the right site gate (if standing outside) is hitting the side curb as it opens, with rather big impact, so it is just adding more force to the pull that eventually tears out the welded on part (this caused the gates failure the last time). Also, it creates quite a bit of noice, especially at night when people are asleep.

Solution? The mechanisms need to be reinforced so the welded on parts will not be just torn out of the gates. Also, opening gap needs to be adjusted, so the gate will not be higging the curb with high impact and noise. If not fixed before the builder leaves, well it seems the gates are being prone to break again and again...
