unit NUMBERS

The flaw with labeling units that causes massive confusion and headache with deliveries of packages to proper doors. Problem that Must be fixed

This ambiguous labeling of units is the most annoying problem, no doubt. And this problem needs to be fixed, preferably ASAP, but make no mistake, this problem must be fixed before the builder leaves the construction.
After the builder leaves the construction, we might be stuck with this problem for good. No doubt, everybody in the community found their packages dropped off at wrong doors, some of us more than others. Some a lot more than the others depending on where they live in the community. Some packages were lost. This problem is complex and needs to be explained in detail, which will be done here soon. It seems the builder does not realize the severity of this problem and for several months the problem was ignored despite MULTIPLE complaints from many people. But only lately, after addressing this issue on this site, the developer promised to look into this issue and this gives hope.

scarce PARKING

A somewhat ridiculous situation with parking when if it was not bad enough to line up all the cars along the road instead of at an angle as it used to be, then it became even worse: to have an unjustifiably long stretch of "NO PARKING FIRE LANE" of the pavement. Problem that Must be fixed

OK, this needs to be explained. Some help from an architect was needed to clarify this, and luckily we have just the man. And yes, we do not want making our community unsafe for fire prevention.

The city of Concord requires a clear access road of minimum 20 feet (See Table D103.4 of Ordinance 2016-23). That's quite possibly why the parking along the drive is parallel to the curb instead of at an angle. Questionable though because it depends on length of a parked vehicle to clear that 20 feet. And it seems like it should clear that 20 feet... Some measurements need to be made to clarify this and to post the test results. But at the same time this totally does not explain why there is a fire lane no parking painted on the curb AFTER the mailbox after just 2 parking spots there. Because the distance from buildings to cars there is almost whopping 30 feet! Many cars could be parked there, LEGALLY. So it is really puzzling as to why the parking is banned over there.
